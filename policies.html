Leave Policy

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: All Permanent Staff

Villa Holdings is committed to supporting a healthy work-life balance for all employees. This policy outlines the types of leave available and the procedures for applying for leave.

Types of Leave

  • Annual Leave: All permanent employees are entitled to 21 working days of paid annual leave per year, increasing by 1 day for every 3 years of service.
  • Sick Leave: Employees are entitled to 30 days of paid sick leave per 3-year cycle. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days.
  • Maternity Leave: Female employees are entitled to 4 months of fully paid maternity leave after 1 year of continuous employment.
  • Paternity Leave: Male employees are entitled to 2 weeks of fully paid paternity leave upon the birth of a child.
  • Compassionate Leave: Up to 5 days of paid leave granted in the event of the death of an immediate family member.
  • Study Leave: Employees enrolled in approved study programmes may apply for up to 10 days of paid study leave per year.

How to Apply for Leave

  • Submit a leave request via the HR portal at least 5 working days in advance (except emergencies).
  • All leave must be approved by your direct line manager before it is confirmed.
  • Leave taken without approval will be treated as unpaid absence.
📌 For more information about leave entitlements, contact HR at [email protected] or visit the Employee Benefits page.

Code of Conduct

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: All Employees

Villa Holdings expects all employees to conduct themselves with the highest standards of professionalism, integrity, and respect at all times — whether in the workplace, at company events, or when representing the company externally.

Expected Behaviour

  • Treat all colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with dignity and respect regardless of their background, role, or seniority.
  • Maintain confidentiality of all company, client, and employee information at all times.
  • Report any conflicts of interest to your line manager or the HR department promptly.
  • Use company resources, systems, and equipment responsibly and only for authorised purposes.
  • Avoid any behaviour that could be construed as harassment, discrimination, or bullying.

Disciplinary Action

  • Violations of this code may result in a formal warning, suspension, or termination depending on severity.
  • All disciplinary matters will be handled in accordance with Villa Holdings' Disciplinary Procedure and applicable labour law.
  • Employees have the right to be heard and represented during any disciplinary process.
📌 To report a code of conduct violation confidentially, email [email protected] or speak to the HR team directly.

Recruitment Policy

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: HR & Hiring Managers

Villa Holdings is an equal opportunity employer. Our recruitment process is designed to be fair, transparent, and free from discrimination. We recruit solely on the basis of merit, skills, and suitability for the role.

Recruitment Principles

  • All vacancies must be advertised internally for a minimum of 5 working days before being opened to external candidates.
  • Job descriptions must be accurate, up to date, and approved by HR before advertising.
  • Selection panels must consist of at least two people, including the line manager and an HR representative.
  • All shortlisted candidates must be assessed against the same criteria using a structured interview process.
  • Reference checks are mandatory for all final candidates before an offer is made.

Equal Opportunity

  • No candidate shall be disadvantaged on the basis of gender, age, race, disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic.
  • Reasonable accommodations will be made for candidates with disabilities during the interview process.

Training & Development Policy

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: All Employees

Villa Holdings is committed to the ongoing learning and professional growth of all employees. We allocate a dedicated budget for training and development activities each financial year.

Training Entitlements

  • Every employee is entitled to a minimum of M5,000 per year for approved training and development activities.
  • Employees may apply for study assistance for formal qualifications relevant to their role.
  • All mandatory training (compliance, safety, HR) must be completed within the stipulated deadlines.
  • Employees who leave within 12 months of completing company-funded training may be required to repay a portion of costs.

How to Apply for Training

  • Submit a Training Request Form to your line manager and HR at least 30 days before the training start date.
  • All training must be pre-approved by both the line manager and the HR department.
  • Upon completion, employees must submit a Training Feedback Report within 5 working days.
📌 Explore available training programmes on our Training & Development page.

Health & Safety Policy

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: All Staff & Visitors

The health, safety, and wellbeing of our employees is of paramount importance to Villa Holdings. We are committed to providing and maintaining a safe working environment for all staff, contractors, and visitors.

Employee Responsibilities

  • Follow all health and safety procedures and instructions provided by management.
  • Report any hazards, accidents, or near-misses to your line manager and the safety officer immediately.
  • Use personal protective equipment (PPE) as required in applicable work areas.
  • Participate in all mandatory health and safety training programmes.
  • Do not operate equipment or machinery you are not trained or authorised to use.

Company Responsibilities

  • Conduct regular risk assessments of all work areas and activities.
  • Provide appropriate safety equipment and training at no cost to employees.
  • Investigate all reported incidents and implement corrective actions promptly.
  • Maintain first aid facilities and ensure trained first aiders are available on every floor.
📌 To report a safety concern, contact the Health & Safety Officer at [email protected].

Data Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 2025 • Applies to: All Employees

Villa Holdings takes the privacy and security of all personal data seriously. This policy outlines how employee and client data is collected, stored, used, and protected in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Data We Collect

  • Personal identification information (name, ID number, contact details).
  • Employment records including contracts, performance reviews, and payroll data.
  • Health information where relevant to occupational health or benefits administration.
  • System access logs and IT usage records for security and compliance purposes.

Employee Rights

  • You have the right to access the personal data Villa Holdings holds about you at any time.
  • You may request correction of inaccurate personal data by contacting the HR department.
  • Personal data will not be shared with third parties without your consent, except where required by law.
  • Data is retained only for as long as necessary and securely destroyed thereafter.
📌 For data access requests or privacy concerns, contact our Data Protection Officer at [email protected].
